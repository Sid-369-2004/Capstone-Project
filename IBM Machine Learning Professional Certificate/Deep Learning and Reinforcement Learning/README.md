## Project Objective

One of the main objectives of this course is to help you gain hands-on experience in communicating insightful and impactful findings to stakeholders. In this project, you will use the tools and techniques you learned throughout this course to apply deep learning to a task of your choosing. 

This can be any Deep Learning application (supervised or unsupervised). You can choose to work on a classification, image, or text application on a dataset that you feel passionate about. You will then tweak your deep learning model to best suit your needs and communicate the insights you found from your model development exercise.

* **Main Deliverable:** A written analytical report (submitted as a PDF) that helps you focus on highlighting your analytical skills, thought process, and next steps.

## Final Project Report Requirements 

Once you have selected a dataset, you will produce the deliverables listed below and submit them for evaluation. Treat this exercise as an opportunity to produce analyses that are ready to highlight your analytical skills for a senior audience, such as the Chief Data Officer or the Head of Analytics at your company.

Your report should focus on presenting findings, insights, and next steps. While you may include some visuals from your code output, the report is intended as a summary of your findings.

### Required Sections in Your Report:

1. **Main Objective of the Analysis:** Specify whether your model will focus on a specific type of Deep Learning or Reinforcement Learning algorithm and the benefits that your analysis brings to the business or stakeholders.
2. **Data Description:** Provide a brief description of the dataset you chose, a summary of its attributes, and an outline of what you are trying to accomplish with this analysis.
3. **Data Exploration & Preprocessing:** Present a brief summary of data exploration and actions taken for data cleaning or feature engineering.
4. **Model Variations & Training:** Summarize the training of at least three variations of the Deep Learning model you selected (e.g., using different hyperparameters, layers, learning rates, epochs, or architectures).
5. **Final Model Recommendation:** A paragraph explaining which of your Deep Learning models you recommend as the final model and why it best fits your needs in terms of accuracy or explainability.
6. **Key Findings & Insights:** Walk your reader through the main findings and takeaways of your modeling exercise aligned with the project objective.
7. **Model Limitations & Next Steps:** Suggest next steps in analyzing this data, highlighting possible flaws/limitations in the model and a plan of action to revisit the analysis (e.g., adding specific features, collecting more data, or trying different modeling techniques).

## Evaluation & Assessment Criteria

Your peer or instructor will review your report from the perspective of a **Chief Data Officer** or the **Head of Analytics** of your team and will assess whether the Deep Learning model you selected best helped you achieve the main objective of your analysis.

### Grading Criteria (The 5 Main Rubric Points):
1. **Data Description:** Does the report include a section describing the data?
2. **Main Objective:** Does the report include a paragraph detailing the main objective(s) of this analysis?
3. **Model Selection & Variations:** Does the report include a section with variations of a Deep Learning model and specify which one best suits the main objective(s) of this analysis?
4. **Key Findings & Insights:** Does the report include a clear and well-presented section with key findings related to the main objective(s) of the analysis?
5. **Limitations & Action Plan:** Does the report highlight possible flaws in the model and a plan of action to revisit this analysis with additional data or different modeling techniques?

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

#### Do I have to come up with my own dataset?
You are highly encouraged to find a dataset you feel really passionate about. This will help you showcase analytical work that truly matches your skills. But if you prefer, you can use some of the datasets from this course.

#### Is it OK to choose the same dataset as someone else?
Yes, more than one person can analyze the same dataset. Most likely your insights will be different from your peers and you will still be able to showcase your own talent as a unique solution.

#### Do I have to train more than 3 different models?
It is highly recommended that you try at least three different model variations to highlight which tool or technique helped you address the main objective of your analysis.

#### Is this an individual assignment?
You can ask for help or assistance on technical issues and general direction of your analysis, but the interpretation of the analytical output and the writing of the report must be your own.
